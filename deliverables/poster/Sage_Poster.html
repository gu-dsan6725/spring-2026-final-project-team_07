Agentic AI Nutritionist

A multi-agent meal-planning system that combines persistent user memory with grounded recipe retrieval and a deterministic audit layer.
Spring 2026 · Poster Session
Tyler Blue  and  Andrew Moy
M.S. Data Science & Analytics, Georgetown University
Published April 24, 2026
In brief

A multi-agent system that produces personalized, nutritionally targeted meal plans through conversation — grounded in a 751-recipe corpus, with a deterministic audit layer outside the LLM.
Perfect on preliminary safety and structure tests; corpus and latency are the remaining limits.

1

The Problem

motivation

Manual trackers like MyFitnessPal place the data-entry burden on the user, while purely generative LLMs struggle to produce reliable nutritional values.

695kcal
Five leading LLMs systematically underestimated daily energy vs. registered-dietitian plans. Bilen et al., 2026 · n = 5 models

We explore a middle path: an agentic architecture that combines persistent memory with grounded retrieval — and isolates safety from LLM inference.

Related work · Angara 2017, Abbas 2026, Guo 2025, Bilen 2026, Khamesian 2024, Papastratis 2024.

System Architecture

figure 1
Figure 1. The Orchestrator (Sonnet 4.6) routes intent to an Intake Agent (Sonnet 4.6, reads/writes Mem0) or the Planning Agent (Haiku 4.5, queries the recipe corpus + SQLite cookbook). The Audit Layer (no LLM) validates every plan for allergens, duplicates, and required slots — looping the Planner up to two times before falling back.

What It Means

findings
01
Grounding eliminates hallucination — by construction.

The system can’t invent meals. The audit is string-matching.

02
Separated agents made debugging tractable.

Narrow prompts, predictable routing, isolated failures.

03
CalorieTarget (0.476) is a corpus problem.

Budget Bytes skews light (213 kcal/srv). USDA corpus closes most of the gap.

04
Latency (0.422) is an infra problem.

2–3 sequential API calls per turn. Parallelize + cache to fix.

9

Conclusion

& references

Takeaway

Separating conversation, retrieval, and validation across specialized agents allowed the system to stay conversational while keeping nutritional accuracy and dietary constraints reliable.
